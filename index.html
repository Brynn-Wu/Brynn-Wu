Hi, I'm
Bingyue Wu

My name is Bingyue Wu (Brynn), from Guangdong province, China. Currently I'm a graduate student in the University of Chinese Academy of Sciences, Beijing, majoring in physical geography.

Ever since my childhood, I have been captivated by the natural world, which led me to wholeheartedly choose Physical Geography for both my bachelor's and master's degrees. Some of my most cherished undergraduate memories are from the various field trips—for courses such as Geomorphology and Plant Geography, where theory came to life.

For my master's, I specialized in palaeoenvironmental reconstruction based on pollen analysis, which required long hours in the laboratory and at the microscope. Even so, I consistently make time for hiking and the outdoors, as these moments continually renew my energy and inspiration.

Studying palaeoenvironment has made me profoundly aware of the unprecedented pressure modern societal development places on our planet. It is this awareness that drives my desire to apply my academic training toward addressing contemporary challenges in human-environment interactions and contributing to sustainable socio-ecological systems.
